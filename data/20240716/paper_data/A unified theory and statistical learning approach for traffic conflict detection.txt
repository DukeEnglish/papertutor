5 Conclusionanddiscussion
Conflictsdonotappearsuddenlyandeveryconflictisaconsequencecontinuedfromsafedriving. Basedon
thisassumption,thisstudyfirstintroducesaunifiedandexplainabletheoryforcollisionriskevaluation,and
proposesastatisticallearningapproachthatappliesthetheoryfordetectingtrafficconflicts. Ourexperiments
onconflictprobabilityestimationandconflictintensityevaluationusereal-worlddataanddemonstratethat
the proposed methodology provides effective collision warnings, is generalisable across datasets, covers
variousconflictsconsistently,anddetectsconflictsinalong-taileddistributionofintensity. Thesefeatures
enableconsistentconflictdetectionandsafetyevaluation,implyingsignificantresearchopportunities. For
example,proximity-basedsurrogatemetricsofconflictscanbeintegratedintoasingletheoreticalframework;
withthestatisticallearningapproach,moreeffectivesurrogatemetricscanbelearnedfromdata;giventhat
collisionriskcanbeconsistentlyevaluatedinvaryingsituations,itispossibletocomparethebehaviourof
road users in different situations and provide consistent assessment on the safety of road infrastructures;
furthermore,combiningthegeneralisabilityanddetectionabilityofhigh-intensityconflicts,safety-critical
eventsthatareneverobservedintrainingdatamaybeeffectivelyidentified,reinforced,andgenerated.
Thecurrentstudyhasseverallimitations,whichcanbeinvestigatedinfutureresearch. First,themethodology
proposedheredoesnotdirectlypredictwhenacollisionwillhappen. Thiscanbeaddressedbyincorporating
trajectorypredictionmodelsandformingamorecomprehensiveframeworkforsafeinteractionplanning.
Second,thistheoryisbasedonproximityanddoesnotindicatetheseverityofapotentialcollision. Collision
severityestimationfocusesontheenergyreleasedbythecollision,whichisnotextremevaluetheorybased,
but more physics driven. As severity is an important aspect of collision avoidance, more exploration is
required. Third, this study uses spatial proximity and trajectory datasets of vehicles for demonstration,
while the methodology can be applied to temporal proximity and to multiple categories of road users
includingpedestriansandcyclists. Futureresearchonmulti-modalinteractionsafetyispromising. Fourth,
the uncertaintyof conflict probabilityestimation andconflict intensity evaluationis not quantifiedin this
study. Suchquantificationcaninformthereliabilityofconflictdetectionandcanbeachievedbyinferring
thedistributionsofproximitycharacteristicparameters,whichistheoreticallyfeasibleastheparametersare
learnedbasedonGaussian. Lastly,althoughweexplainedlearningtherepresentationofinteractioncontext
asakeysubtask,thisstudydoesnotconductrepresentationlearning. Theoretically,thismethodologycan
include almost all the information one can collect to describe the interactions between road users. This
cangobeyondmovements,includingroadlayouts,weather,individualcharacteristicsofroadusers,etc. It
needstonote,however,thatthemoreinformationgetsinvolved,themorediversedataisrequiredtotrainan
effectiveunifiedmetric. Giventhatthismethodologyissuitableforlearningfromaccumulativeevidence,
moreresearchcanbeexpandeduponcontinuallearning,whichisofinteresttoboththefieldsoftrafficsafety
andautonomousdriving.
